What is Skin Cancer?

Skin cancer is the abnormal growth of skin cells, typically caused by excessive exposure to ultraviolet (UV) rays. It is the most common form of cancer worldwide.

Types of Skin Cancer

Melanoma

Highly aggressive, often looks like a mole. Early detection is critical.

Basal Cell Carcinoma

The most common type, often appears as a pearly bump on sun-exposed areas.

Squamous Cell Carcinoma

Appears as a red, scaly patch that may bleed or crust. Treatable if caught early.

Common Signs & Symptoms

  • ✓ A sore that doesn't heal
  • ✓ New growth or mole that changes in size or color
  • ✓ Irregular borders or multiple colors in a mole
  • ✓ Itching, tenderness, or bleeding in a skin lesion

How to Examine Your Skin

  1. Use a full-length mirror in a well-lit room.
  2. Check your entire body including scalp, back, and feet.
  3. Use a hand mirror for hard-to-see areas.
  4. Photograph moles to track changes over time.

Preventing Skin Cancer

  • 🌞 Avoid direct sun between 10 AM and 4 PM
  • 🧴 Use SPF 30+ sunscreen daily
  • 🧢 Wear protective clothing and hats
  • 🚫 Avoid tanning beds and UV lamps
  • 👀 Regularly check skin for changes
